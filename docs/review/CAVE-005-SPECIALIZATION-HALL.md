# CAVE-005 specialization hall

The continuous flat-floor interior begins behind the open entrance: a lit stone corridor leads to a higher, wider hall. Five static, collidable specialization doors use local DynamicTexture stone plaques: Runtime Threat Detection, Linux Kernel & eBPF, Embedded & IoT Systems, OTA & Device Fleets, and Sensor Data Pipelines. They remain CLOSED and non-interactive; their stable IDs/positions are future room-extension anchors. No room content exists beyond them.

Automated Chromium validation used real pointer-lock keyboard input from exterior spawn through the main door and into the corridor. Frame summaries were constrained by headless rendering (roughly 48–50 ms); counts remained one loop and six listeners across reset. CAVE-006 should improve plaque readability and hall containment polish before presentation.
