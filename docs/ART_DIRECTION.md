# Art direction

The exterior is a grounded mountainside threshold: layered asymmetric rock masses rise above and around the arch, embedding the oak-and-iron door in a darker cave mouth. Cool overcast fill and a tightly scoped warm guide retain mystery while keeping the entrance readable. Sparse moss-green grass and low rock scatter soften nearby stone without turning the entrance into a forest.

The corridor deliberately keeps reliable flat structural collision beneath visual rock shelves, floor-edge ridges, and offset protrusions. The hall lifts the visual hierarchy with buttresses, a modest dais, framed/reinforced oak doors, dark bronze plaques, and a restrained warm/cool light pool. The Linux/eBPF annex extends this language with a compact stone shell, subdued bronze plaques, and warm technical study lighting—without changing into a sci-fi display. All geometry, materials, labels, and atmosphere remain procedural/local.
